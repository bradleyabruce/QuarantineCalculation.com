Direct Infections

This is an estimate of the number of individuals you could potentially infect directly if you left quarantine while contagious with COVID-19 over the course of 30 days. The table shows the amount of direct infections you are stopping by social distancing. The line graph below shows three separate data points:

  • The number of direct infections that would occur if an infected individual stopped social distancing now.
  • The number of direct infections that would occur if an infected individual stopped social distancing 15 days ago.
  • The number of direct infections that would occur if an infected individual stopped social distancing 15 days from now.


Assumptions:

  • 3% Infection rate.
  • You are contagious for 38 days (the upper limit observed in Wuhan, China during the initial outbreak of COVID-19).
  • Individuals in urban enviroments will come in contact with 30 new individuals per day.
  • Individuals in rural enviroments will come in contact with 10 new individuals per day.


Indirect Infections

This is an estimate of the number of individuals you could potential infect indirectly (community spread) while contagious with COVID-19 over the course of 6 months.





Assumptions:

  • 3% Infection rate.
  • Every newly infected individual has 8 to 15 days of viral incubation where they are not contagious.
  • After their incubation period, each individual is contagious for 8 to 37 days when is virus is actively producing new nRNA.
  • When an idividual is infected, they have a 38% chance of having general symptoms, 35% chance of having severe symptoms, and 28% of having critial symptoms
  • Individuals with severe symptioms have a 22% chance of dying per day after 8 to 20 days of showing symptoms
  • Individuals with critical symptoms have a 78% change of dying per day after 8 to 20 days of showing symptoms
  • Individuals in urban enviroments will come in contact with 30 new individuals per day.
  • Individuals in rural enviroments will come in contact with 10 new individuals per day.
