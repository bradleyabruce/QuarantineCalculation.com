Indirect Infections

Community spread is the spread of a disease through a population where the source of the infection is unknown. This occurs when individuals begin spreading a disease to others in great enough number to make it impossible to know who infected who. This type of spread grows exponentially because for every new individual that becomes infected, the rate at which infection occurs also increases. This is an estimate of the number of individuals you could potential indirectly infect and kill through others while contagious with COVID-19 over the course of 2 months.





Assumptions:

  • 3% Infection rate.
  • Every newly infected individual has 8 to 15 days of viral incubation where they are not contagious.
  • After their incubation period, each individual is contagious for 8 to 37 days when is virus is actively producing new nRNA.
  • When an idividual is infected, they have a 38% chance of having general symptoms, 35% chance of having severe symptoms, and 28% of having critial symptoms.
  • Individuals with general symptioms do not have a chance of dying.
  • Individuals with severe symptioms have a 22% chance of dying per day after 8 to 20 days of showing symptoms.
  • Individuals with critical symptoms have a 78% change of dying per day after 8 to 20 days of showing symptoms.
  • Individuals in urban enviroments will come in contact with 30 new individuals per day.
  • Individuals in rural enviroments will come in contact with 10 new individuals per day.

About Social Distancing Calculator

Quarantine Calculation uses a variety of data sources to calculate the most likely possible infection rates. Epidemology, or the study of the spread of diseases, is difficult in any situation, but even more so during an active pandemic due to a variety of factors. All results on this website are estimates based on observed data from the initial outbreak of COVID-19 in Wuhan, China and should not be taken as scientific fact. This website's purpose is to simply show how the exponential spead of a disease can affect many people over time.
